01 · Strategic Brief

A smoke alarm for protocol upgrades.

Upgrade Siren is not a SaaS product. It is a piece of public infrastructure that warns DAO voters and venture investors when a named Ethereum protocol upgrade changes what they are trusting. The brand exists to make one moment unmistakable: an ENS name resolves, the implementation flips, and the verdict turns from green to red in under five seconds.

Mission

Make proxy-upgrade risk legible to the people who actually vote and fund. Lead with evidence, not claims. Be loud when it matters, silent otherwise.

Positioning

A public smoke alarm for Ethereum upgrades — civic, evidence-led, anti-extractive. Not an audit firm. Not a security platform. Not an agent. Just an alarm that tells you whether to trust the next click.

Audience

  • DAO voters and delegates — non-auditors deciding on upgrade proposals, often on mobile.
  • Hackathon judges and sponsors — Sourcify, ENS, ETHPrague Future Society. Three-minute booth glance.
  • Wallet and explorer integrators — secondary B2B, must look API-clean and embeddable.

Lead attributes

  • Alarm clarity. Verdict legible at three meters. Color paired with glyph at every size.
  • Public-good civic. Reads like a fire-safety pamphlet a city would publish.
  • Security-honest. Never says "trusted" or "secure". Only "verified" and "evidence-backed".
  • Live and current. Every screen feels like a real-time signal, not a static report.

What the brand must do

  • Make the verdict triade — SAFE / REVIEW / SIREN — the single most-recognizable primitive.
  • Survive booth Wi-Fi and judge fatigue: the green→red flip must read across the room.
  • Hold up under deuteranopia and protanopia. Color alone is never the signal.
  • Stay civic, not corporate. Treat the manual as a public document, not marketing collateral.

The five-second moment

The brand-defining demo beat: a protocol name resolves through ENS, the proxy implementation changes underneath, Sourcify evidence loads, and the screen flips from green SAFE to red SIREN. This is the brand. Every other deliverable — type, icons, components, posters — exists to support that one motion.

02 · Logo System

Three directions. One mark.

Three logo directions were explored. Each had to answer: does it survive at favicon size, does it read at three meters, and does it carry the verdict triade without becoming a literal cartoon siren? The recommended primary is Direction A — the Triade Stack.

A · Triade Stack
Three glyphs (check / bar / siren-bar) stacked beside an "S". Reads as a public-safety pictogram. Verdict triade is encoded directly in the mark.

→ Recommended primary.
B · Wave-S
An "S" formed from a soundwave with three spark marks above. Strongest at large sizes, but the spark detail collapses below 24px. Reserved for poster-scale only.
C · Beacon
A square with a cut corner showing two lit indicators. The cut signals "state can flip". Strong concept; the concept fights legibility — too many people read it as a sticky-note.

Why Direction A is primary

  • The mark is the product. The verdict triade is the canonical primitive; encoding it in the mark eliminates the gap between brand and product.
  • Holds at 16×16. Tested at favicon size: glyphs reduce to colored bars, the "S" stays legible.
  • Civic vocabulary. Reads as a public-safety pictogram (recycle marks, accessibility marks, hazard plates), not as a tech logo.
  • Color-blind safe. The three glyph shapes are distinct under deuteranopia and protanopia.

Color-blind simulation report

Verdict colors were checked under simulated deuteranopia (red-green) and protanopia (red-weak). Findings:

  • Under deuteranopia, verdict-safe shifts toward dull yellow-green and verdict-review shifts toward muddy yellow — the two are visually similar at small sizes.
  • Under protanopia, verdict-siren darkens significantly — it remains distinct from verdict-review, but loses alarm "punch" without motion or glyph reinforcement.
  • Mitigation (mandatory). Every verdict color is paired with a unique glyph shape: ✓ check, ▤ horizontal bars, ▮ vertical alarm bar. The system never renders a verdict color without its glyph.
  • The SIREN state additionally pulses (1.1s ease-in-out, single play) to signal alarm via motion, not just hue.

Accent justification — cyan over electric blue

The accent (#00B8D9) is reserved exclusively for resolved ENS names and active links. Cyan was chosen over the more common electric blue for two reasons. First, electric blue is the default "web3" hue; using it would code Upgrade Siren into a category we explicitly reject. Second, cyan sits cleanly outside the red-amber-green verdict triade in hue space, eliminating any chance of misreading a link as a verdict.

Motion specification — the green→red flip

  • Trigger. A new implementation address appears in the EIP-1967 slot during an active session, OR the user clicks PLAY in the booth demo.
  • Duration. 480ms total, single play. Easing: cubic-bezier(0.2, 0.8, 0.2, 1.0).
  • Color interpolation. Background and border interpolate in HSL space from safe-surface #062A1B to siren-surface #2D0B09; verdict word and glyph from verdict-safe #00D67A to verdict-siren #FF3B30. Both transitions share the same easing and start frame to keep the moment unified.
  • Glyph swap. Check fades out 0–160ms (opacity only). Siren bar fades in 240–400ms. The glyph slot itself does not move.
  • Word swap. Word "SAFE" cross-fades to "SIREN" between 200–400ms. No slide, no scale — only opacity, to keep the type stable at booth viewing distance.
  • Pulse. On arrival, the SIREN card pulses once: 1.1s ease-in-out, single play, halo at rgba(255,59,48,0.35).
  • Audio. Optional, off by default, single 280ms square-wave chirp at 880 Hz on SIREN arrival. Off-default because public spaces.
  • Reduced motion. If prefers-reduced-motion: reduce is set, color interpolation runs at full 480ms but the pulse is skipped.

12 · Voice & Tone

Lead with evidence. Lead with verdict. Never lead with AI.

Upgrade Siren is a civic tool. The voice is brutal-direct, evidence-first, and free of marketing fluff. It never overclaims safety. It never says "trusted by". It never uses emoji.

Always

  • "verified" · "evidence-backed" · "signed by"
  • "verdict from public chain state"
  • "signed by upgrade-siren:owner"
  • "the implementation changed at tx 0x612d…"
  • "do not approve until re-signed"
  • Plain English. Short sentences. Imperative when it matters.

Never

  • "trusted by" / "secured by" / "audited by"
  • "AI-powered" / "agent" / "scanner" / "trust layer"
  • "platform" / "ecosystem" / "Web3 infrastructure"
  • Emoji of any kind, including in social copy and pitch slides.
  • Localized versions of "No source, no upgrade." for the hackathon.
  • Marketing claims that cannot be backed by an on-chain citation.

13 · Anti-Patterns

What the brand must never look like.

If a draft starts looking like any of these, it has drifted. Pull back to the verdict triade and the civic vocabulary.

Corporate SaaS dashboard

Three-column metric cards over a customer-logo wall. "Trusted by 200+ DAOs." Gradient mesh background. We are not a vendor. We are an alarm.

AI-auditor cliché

Magnifying glass over scrolling code. Robot-with-shield mascot. Chat-bubble UI asking "Should I approve this upgrade?" We do not produce opinions. We surface evidence.

Web3 default · purple-blue

Purple-to-blue gradient. Glassmorphism cards. Geometric NFT-style hero illustration. Metallic sheen on the wordmark. None of this. The brand is matte, civic, and honest.

"Agent OS" / "trust layer"

A floating orb representing "intelligence". Slogans like "the trust layer for upgrades" or "the agent OS for governance". Neither term ever appears in our copy.

Logo do / don't

DO
  • Use the mark on its own at small sizes (≤24px).
  • Pair the mark with the wordmark at 1× clear-space minimum.
  • Use the monochrome mark for single-ink contexts.
  • Treat the tagline as part of the lockup and never edit it.
DON'T
  • Don't recolor the verdict glyphs to match a host site.
  • Don't apply gradients, drop shadows, or bevels to the mark.
  • Don't translate "No source, no upgrade." for the hackathon.
  • Don't crop the mark — the box is part of the glyph.
  • Don't use the mark as a decorative pattern.
