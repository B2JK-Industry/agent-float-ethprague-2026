System rationale

Why sequential, not side-by-side. A judge does not allocate 1.2M EUR by squinting at three columns. A reviewer reads one case at a time, in full, and writes one verdict before moving to the next. Bench follows that ritual: one applicant fills the screen, the docket queues the rest.

The 30-second moment becomes the 30-second arc. Step 1 sets a baseline — every source verified, fast-track. Step 2 introduces ambiguity — can we trust, but we lack artefacts. Step 3 lands the alarm — signature mismatch, replaced citation, fictional registry. The contrast emerges through time, which is what makes it a review rather than a comparison.

The Score Math sheet is the receipt. Every headline number — 87, 52, 28 — opens its own auditor's worksheet. Substance, Provenance & Seniority, Relevance to Brief each break into named line items with earned/max counts; Trust is a per-source weighted ledger. The final arithmetic is shown, not hidden: ( substance + provenance + relevance ) × trust = score.

What stays from Contract Risk. The siren red is reserved for the moment Bench refuses to discount — invalid signature, replaced citation, fictional citation. Everything else lives in cool neutrals (tier letters, trust multipliers) and a separate epistemic gradient for confidence. The brand promise extends: No proof, no allocation.
